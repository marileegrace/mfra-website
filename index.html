What is MFRA?

Multi-Factor Return Attribution breaks a stock's daily return into four components to answer: "Why is this stock moving?"

  • Cyan — How much of the move comes from the broad market (SPY)
  • Orange — How much comes from the sector (e.g., XLK), net of market
  • Green — How much comes from the sub-sector (e.g., SOXX), net of sector
  • Purple — The residual — the stock's own idiosyncratic momentum that can't be explained by market, sector, or sub-sector

We use a 42-day rolling regression (OLS) to estimate betas for each factor, then multiply beta × factor return to get each component's daily contribution. The last 10 days are summed for the "10d" values you see in the table.

How to Read the Columns

  • Ticker — Stock symbol
  • Sector / SubSec — The ETFs used as benchmarks. Market is always SPY.
  • Quality — "Best" (green background) or "Acceptable". See quality section below.
  • R² — Model fit. How well the 3 factors explain the stock's moves. Sweet spot: 0.40–0.70. Too low = noisy model, too high = stock just tracks the index.
  • B Mkt / B Sec / B Sub — Beta (sensitivity) to each factor. e.g., B Mkt = 1.5 means the stock moves 1.5% for every 1% SPY move.
  • Purple 10d — Sum of idiosyncratic daily contributions over the last 10 bars. This is the key signal — positive purple = the stock has its own momentum beyond what the market/sector explains.
  • Cyan 10d — Market contribution (10d sum)
  • Orange 10d — Sector contribution (10d sum, net of market)
  • Green 10d — Sub-sector contribution (10d sum, net of sector)
  • Prp+ — How many of the last 10 daily residual bars were positive (e.g., 7/10)
  • Spike% — The largest single bar as a percentage of total positive purple. Flags one-off moves vs. sustained momentum.
  • Wind — Sector tailwind. "Yes" if orange or green ≥ 0 (sector/sub-sector not dragging the stock down).
  • Cyn% — Cyan's share of total positive contributions. If too high, the stock is just riding the market.

The 5 Entry Criteria

All 5 must pass for a stock to qualify as an opportunity:

  • 1. R² in Range (0.40–0.70) — The model must explain 40–70% of variance. Below 0.40 = too much noise, the decomposition isn't reliable. Above 0.70 = the stock is just tracking its ETF — no alpha edge.
  • 2. Purple Sustained (5+/10) — At least 5 of the last 10 daily residual bars must be positive. This filters for sustained idiosyncratic momentum, not just a one-day pop.
  • 3. No Purple Spike (≤60%) — No single daily bar can be more than 60% of total positive purple. Prevents a single gap-up day from looking like momentum.
  • 4. Sector Tailwind — Either orange (sector) or green (sub-sector) 10d sum must be ≥ 0. We want the sector or sub-sector at least not dragging the stock down.
  • 5. Cyan < 70% — Market contribution can't be more than 70% of total positive contributions. If cyan dominates, the stock is just riding a broad market rally.

Quality: Best vs. Acceptable

Stocks that pass all 5 criteria are further classified:

  • Best (green row) — R² between 0.50–0.65 (tighter sweet spot), 7+ purple positive bars (out of 10), AND positive sector tailwind (orange or green > 0). These are the highest-conviction setups.
  • Acceptable — Passes all 5 criteria but doesn't meet the stricter "Best" thresholds. Still a valid setup, just slightly less ideal.

What Are Near Misses?

A near miss is a stock that failed exactly one of the 5 entry criteria. These are worth watching because:

  • They're close to qualifying and may pass tomorrow
  • The "Failed" column tells you which single rule they missed
  • The "Note" column gives the specific value that failed (e.g., "R²=0.38 outside [0.4, 0.7]")

Stocks that fail 2+ criteria are not shown — they're too far from qualifying to be actionable.

Using the Live Scan Tab

The SCAN tab lets you analyze any ticker on demand:

  • Enter a ticker symbol (e.g., AAPL, NVDA, COIN)
  • The scanner auto-detects the stock's sector and suggests the appropriate sector ETF and sub-sector ETF
  • You can override either dropdown before scanning
  • Results show the same columns as the daily scan, plus a pass/fail verdict with details
  • The scan fetches ~6 months of price data from Yahoo Finance and runs the full MFRA analysis in your browser
